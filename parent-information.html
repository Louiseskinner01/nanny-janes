Food Menu

The nursery is committed to ensuring that all pupils have the opportunity to enjoy and benefit from a nutritious lunch. We take pride in using fresh ingredients, with meals prepared and cooked onsite. The food children eat at nursery plays a vital role in their development. A well-balanced diet not only supports overall health but also helps children maintain focus, energy, and readiness to learn. Establishing healthy eating habits at a young age gives children the knowledge and foundation they need for the future. Our menu changes weekly and is added to the app for you to view.

Nursery Charges & Funding

We aim to keep our fees transparent and our funding options easy to understand, so you can plan childcare with confidence.

Our Fees:

Our nursery fees are £12 per hour. We offer flexible options, including full-day and part-day sessions, to suit different family needs.

Fees include:

  • High-quality childcare and early years education.
  • Nutritious meals and snacks.
  • Access to all learning resources and activities.

Funding:

We support families in accessing government-funded childcare schemes, including:

  • 15 hours funded childcare for eligible 2-year-olds
  • 15 hours universal funding for all 3- and 4-year-olds
  • 30 hours extended funding for eligible working families
  • Funding is typically available during term time, and we can discuss options for stretching these hours across the year.

Tax-Free Childcare:

We accept payments through the UK Government’s Tax-Free Childcare scheme, which can help reduce your childcare costs.

Getting Started:

If you would like help understanding your eligibility or calculating fees, our team is happy to guide you through the process.


Safeguarding

SAFEGUARDING STATEMENT

We have a legal duty to report any concerns about a child’s safety to Children’s Social Care. If staff have concerns about a child, we will usually discuss these with you first. If needed, we may then refer the matter to Children’s Social Care.

Policies

We have a range of policies in place to create a safe, fair and caring environment. We want children to feel safe, know that staff care about them, and feel confident that staff will listen to them.

First Aid

All staff receive first aid training every year. Some staff also have specialist paediatric first aid training. First aid kits are available throughout the nursery. If a child becomes ill or has an accident, staff follow clear procedures to care for them.

Onsite security

Nanny Janes Nursery provides a secure environment. There is CCTV in place and controlled access, so only authorised staff can enter areas where children are present.

Safer Recruitment

We follow a thorough recruitment process. All staff have a full DBS check carried out by the Local Authority.

Outings & Risk Assessments

Staff are trained to carry out risk assessments. These are completed for all outings, and clear procedures are followed.


We have several safeguarding policies available for parents and carers. Some can be found on our website, and others are available on request from reception.

SEND: Supporting Children with Additional Needs

We provide a caring and inclusive environment where every child is supported to thrive. We understand that all children are different, so we tailor our support to meet each child’s individual needs. We work closely with parents, carers, and other professionals to make sure children receive the right support.

We Offer:

  • Individual support for each child.
  • Strong partnerships with families.
  • Help from outside professionals when needed.
  • An inclusive setting where all children feel welcome.
  • Our staff are experienced and continue to develop their skills to support children with additional needs.

We aim to create a safe, supportive place where every child feels valued and included.
